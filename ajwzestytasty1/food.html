10 Restaurants to Eat At Before You Die

Introduction

Not to be morbid or frightening to anyone, but these restaurants are the ones you need to go to before you die. Indeed, you'll be going back for more.

Restaurants in the United States

Bonchon (Korean Spicy Fried Chicken) $$

With 88 restaurants in the U.S. and many more in Asia and the Middle East, Bonchon is a Korean friend chicken franchise. Its name meaning "My Hometown," their chicken comes with two options, spicy and soy garlic, attract friends and family from all over the world.

Address: bonchon.com



Pomegranate Restaurant (Russian-Gregorian) $$

Pomegranate is a Russian-Gregorian Restaurant in San Diego. Its delicious khachapuri, or boats of hot cheese mixed with egg, chicken tabaka, and Babushka's Surprise, a dessert of fresh-baked apple filled with fruit, walnuts, honey, and topped with ice cream.

Address: 2312 El Cajon Blvd, San Diego, CA 92104
Website: pomegranatesd.com



Rock N' Jenny's Subs (Sandwich Shop) $

Rock N' Jenny's Subs is a family-run deli with outdoor seating serving Italian cold cuts & other sandwich choices. The Hot Pastrami sandwich is delish with toasty, crispy bread, fresh lettuce, flavorful pastrami, and the italian sauce is amazing. Service is also extremely friendly but lines can be long depending on the time of day.

Address: 1044 W Valley Pkwy, Escondido, CA 92025
Website: rocknjennysitaliansubs.com



Tacos El Gordo (Taco Shop) $

Open until 1 in the morning, Tacos El Gordo is one of the best taco shops for a Mexican taste. Featured taco is my favorite Adobada with succulent spicy meat on a corn tortilla. However, there is a huge lunch time rush and very long lines.

Address: 3265 Palm Ave, San Diego, CA 92154
Website: tacoselgordobc.com
